This site is an open content ecosystem, designed as a decentralized information protocol about the I Ching. To ensure maximum dissemination and adoption, the intellectual property of the site is structured in independent layers, each with its own legal regime.

---

## Layer 1 · Original German Text (Public Domain)

The base texts of the I Ching used on this site correspond to the German translation by **Richard Wilhelm**, originally published in 1924 under the title *I Ging, das Buch der Wandlungen*.

Richard Wilhelm passed away in 1930. His original work in German is in the **public domain** in the vast majority of jurisdictions worldwide (where copyright lasts for the life of the author plus 70 or 80 years). Therefore, these texts are not subject to copyright and belong to the common heritage of humanity.

Original authorship is attributed to Richard Wilhelm for each hexagram and base text used.

## Layer 3 · Public Domain Content and Traditional Knowledge

This site includes content that is **not subject to copyright** as it is traditional knowledge or public domain:

- **The 64 hexagrams of the I Ching** and their base texts (known since antiquity).
- **The Earlier Heaven (Fu Xi) and Later Heaven (King Wen) arrangements** of the trigrams.
- **The Advanced Correspondence Table**: The correspondences between trigrams, elements, directions, seasons, family, animals, body parts, etc., are traditional knowledge documented in classical I Ching literature for centuries. The presentation of these correspondences in table format on this site is a compilation of public domain knowledge, not an original work subject to copyright.

This content may be used freely by anyone without restrictions.
