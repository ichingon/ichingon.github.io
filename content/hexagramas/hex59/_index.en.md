### Trigrams
* Above: Xùn, the Gentle, Wind
* Below: Kǎn, the Abysmal, Water

The wind blowing over the water scatters it, dissolving it into foam and mist. This implies that when life energy becomes stagnant within a person - an internal block symbolized by the dangerous nature of the lower trigram - gentleness can gently scatter and dissolve the rigidity.

---

## The Judgment
> Dispersion. Success.
> The king approaches his temple.
> It furthers one to cross the great water.
> Perseverance furthers.

The text of this hexagram closely resembles that of Hexagram 45, Ts'ui (Gathering Together). In that hexagram, the focus is on gathering what is scattered, just as water collects into lakes upon the earth. Here, the focus is on dispersing and dissolving the egoism that separates us. Dispersion shows the very path that leads back to gathering, which explains why the texts are so similar.

Overcoming the egoism that divides humanity requires deep spiritual forces. The shared celebration of great religious festivals and worship services - which simultaneously expressed the interconnectedness and social structure of the family and the state - was the means used by the great rulers. Through sacred music and the splendor of ceremonies, they stirred hearts in a collective wave of feeling, awakening a consciousness of the common origin of all beings. In this way, divisions were overcome and rigid isolation dissolved. 

Another powerful means is working together on great common undertakings. By setting a great goal for the collective will, all divisive elements dissolve in the pursuit of that objective - much like passengers on a ship crossing a great river, who must unite in common effort to succeed.

However, only someone who remains free from selfish ulterior motives, standing firm in justice and constancy, is capable of dissolving the hardness of egoism in this way.

---

## The Image
> The wind drives over the water: the image of Dispersion.
> Thus the kings of old sacrificed to the Lord and built temples.

In autumn and winter, water begins to harden and freeze into ice. When the mild breezes of spring arrive, the rigidity thaws, and the scattered ice floes reunite. The same dynamics apply to the spirit of a people. Hardness and selfishness cause the heart to freeze, and this rigidity isolates it from everyone else. Egoism and greed alienate people. Therefore, a devout reverence must touch the human heart. It must be dissolved in sacred awe of the eternal - shaken by the awareness of the common creator of all beings, and unified by the power of community feelings during the sacred celebration of divine worship.

## The Individual Lines

### Six at the beginning means:
> He brings help with the strength of a horse.
> Good fortune.

Here, the focus is on overcoming the first signs of separation before the division is fully complete - scattering the clouds before the storm and rain arrive. In times when subtle shifts in mood occur and mutual misunderstandings begin to take root, one must act quickly and decisively to dissolve these misgivings and dispel mutual distrust.

### Nine in the second place means:
> At the dissolution, he runs to his support.
> Remorse disappears.

When you discover within yourself the beginnings of alienation from others, misanthropy, or ill-will, you must actively disperse these inner blockages. You must rouse yourself internally and hasten toward your true support. A person's ultimate support never lies in hatred, but always in a balanced, just, and benevolent view of humanity. By regaining this open, clear perspective on mankind and scattering all melancholic discontent, any reason for remorse vanishes.

### Six in the third place means:
> He dissolves his self. No remorse.

At times, the task at hand can become so demanding that you can no longer think of yourself. You must set your own persona entirely aside, dispersing everything that the ego wishes to gather around itself to separate you from others. Only on the foundation of a great renunciation do you gain the strength for great achievements. By placing your goal outside of yourself in a great cause, you can firmly establish this standpoint.

### Six in the fourth place means:
> He dissolves his bond with his group. Supreme good fortune.
> Dispersion leads to accumulation.
> This is something ordinary people do not think of.

When working on a task that affects the greater good, you must set all private friendships aside. Only by rising above partisan interests can you achieve something truly decisive. Whoever dares to renounce what is near will win what is distant. However, you must have a vast overview of life's interconnected patterns - the kind of vision only exceptional individuals attain - to truly understand this standpoint.

### Nine in the fifth place means:
> Loud cries dissolve like sweat.
> Dissolution! A king dwells without blame.

In times of general dissolution and separation, a great idea serves as the focal point for recovery. Just as a physical illness reaches its turning point and clears through a breaking sweat, a great, inspiring idea acts as a true liberation in times of general stagnation. It gives people something around which they can rally, led by a commanding figure who can dissolve all misunderstandings.

### Nine at the top means:
> He dissolves his blood.
> Departing, keeping at a distance, going out is without blame.

Dissolving one's blood means dispersing whatever might lead to bloodshed and wounds, thereby avoiding danger. This does not imply that a person merely evades difficulties for their own sake. Rather, it means saving one's people - helping them depart before danger strikes, keeping them at a distance from an existing peril, and finding a way out of a crisis that has already engulfed them. In this manner, one does what is right.
