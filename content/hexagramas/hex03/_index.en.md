### Trigrams
* Above: Kǎn, the Abysmal, Water
* Below: Zhèn, the Arousing, Thunder

The name of the hexagram, Zhūn, originally represents a blade of grass pushing up against an obstacle as it sprouts from the earth. From this arises the meaning of difficulty at the beginning. The hexagram indicates how heaven and earth bring forth individual beings. It is their first encounter, which is inherently accompanied by difficulties. The lower trigram, Zhèn, is the Arousing; its movement is directed upward, and its image is thunder. The upper trigram, Kǎn, is the Abysmal, the dangerous; its movement is directed downward, and its image is rain. The situation therefore points to a dense, chaotic abundance. Thunder and rain fill the air. However, the chaos eventually clears. The upward movement, even as the abysmal element descends, finally breaks free from danger. The built-up tensions release during the thunderstorm, and everything breathes a sigh of relief.

---

## The Judgment
> Difficulty at the beginning brings supreme success.
> Perseverance furthers.
> One should not undertake anything.
> It is advantageous to appoint helpers.

Times of growth are filled with difficulties. It is like a first birth. Yet these difficulties arise from the sheer abundance of what is striving for form. Everything is in motion; therefore, despite the inherent danger, there is a prospect of great success if one maintains perseverance. When such times of beginning come as a destiny, everything is still unformed and dark. For this reason, one must wait, as any premature action could bring failure. It is equally critical not to remain isolated. One needs helpers to master the chaos together. This does not mean watching the events unfold passively; rather, one must join in, offering encouragement and guidance throughout.

---

## The Image
> Clouds and thunder: the image of difficulty at the beginning.
> Thus the superior person sorts out and brings order.

Clouds and thunder are represented by specific structural lines, which implies that order is already inherent within the chaos of early difficulties. Thus, during such times of beginning, the superior person must organize and structure the confusing abundance, much like unraveling silk threads from a tangled skein and binding them into strands. To find one's way within the infinite, one must learn both to distinguish and to connect.

---

## The Lines

### Nine at the beginning means:
> Hesitation and hindrance.
> It furthers to remain persevering.
> It furthers to appoint helpers.

When encountering a hindrance at the start of an enterprise, one must not attempt to force progress, but should instead pause cautiously. However, one must not allow oneself to be destabilized, but must steadfastly keep the goal in mind. It is essential to seek the right assistance. Such helpers are found only by interacting with people with humility and avoiding arrogance. Only in this way do people willingly align with you, allowing you to tackle the difficulties together.

### Six in the second place means:
> Difficulties pile up.
> Horse and wagon separate.
> He is no robber,
> He comes to woo in due time.
> The maiden is chaste, she does not pledge herself.
> Ten years, then she pledges herself.

One finds oneself in difficulty and restraint. Then suddenly a turn occurs, as if someone arrived with a horse and wagon and unhitched them. This event comes so unexpectedly that one suspects the newcomer to be a robber. Gradually it becomes clear that he has no evil intentions, but seeks a friendly alliance and offers relief. Yet the offer is rejected because it does not come from the right source; instead, one waits until the time is fulfilled - ten years signify a completed cycle, a fulfilled time. Then normal conditions naturally return, and one can unite with the intended partner.

Under the image of a bride who remains faithful to her beloved during severe conflicts, advice is offered for a specific life situation: when sudden relief is offered by an unrelated party during times of difficulty and hindrance, one must remain cautious and avoid taking on obligations that such assistance would entail, as this would compromise freedom of decision. By waiting for the right time, peaceful conditions will return, and what was hoped for will be achieved.[^1]

### Six in the third place means:
> Whoever hunts the deer without the forester only loses his way in the forest.
> The superior person understands the signs of the time and prefers to desist.
> To go on brings humiliation.

Hunting in an unfamiliar forest without a guide leads to getting lost. One must not attempt to break out of current difficulties blindly and without guidance. Fate cannot be deceived. Premature striving without the necessary direction leads to failure and shame. Therefore, the superior person, recognizing the seeds of what is to come, prefers to renounce a desire rather than bring about failure and humiliation by trying to force it.

### Six in the fourth place means:
> Horse and wagon separate.
> Strive for union.
> To go brings good fortune.
> Everything acts to further.

One is in a position where action is required, yet one's own strength is insufficient. However, an opportunity to connect presents itself. It is crucial to seize it. One must not be held back by false pride or misplaced reluctance. It is a sign of inner clarity when one overcomes inertia to take the first step, even if it involves a certain degree of self-denial. In a difficult situation, there is no shame in accepting help. If the right helper is found, everything turns out well.

### Nine in the fifth place means:
> Difficulties in granting blessings.
> Small perseverance brings good fortune,
> Great perseverance brings misfortune.

One finds oneself in a situation where it is impossible to express good intentions in a way that makes them manifest and understood. Others interpose themselves and distort everything one does. Under these circumstances, one must proceed cautiously and gradually. One should not attempt to force grand designs, as such things succeed only when general trust is already established. Only through quiet, faithful, and conscientious work can one gradually clear up the situation and dissolve the hindrances.

### Six at the top means:
> Horse and wagon separate.
> Bloody tears pour forth.

Some people find the initial difficulties too overwhelming. They get stuck and cannot find their way through. They drop their hands and give up the struggle. Such a resignation is among the saddest of outcomes. Therefore, Confucius remarks on this: "Bloody tears pour forth: one should not do this continuously."
