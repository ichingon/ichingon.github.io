### Trigrams
* Above: Kǎn, the Abysmal, Water
* Below: Duì, the Joyous, Lake

A lake occupies a limited space. When more water flows into it, it overflows. Therefore, limits must be set. The image shows water below and water above, with the firmament of heaven acting as a boundary between them.

The Chinese word for limitation originally refers to the firm joints that divide a bamboo stalk. In daily life, it signifies economy, which sets fixed boundaries for expenditure. In the moral sphere, it represents the clear limits that the superior person sets for their actions - the boundaries of loyalty and selflessness.

---

## The Judgment
> Limitation. Success.
> Bitter limitation must not be persevered in.

Boundaries are demanding, but they achieve results. By practicing economy in everyday life, one is prepared for times of scarcity. Exercising restraint saves one from embarrassment. Furthermore, limitations are indispensable in maintaining the order of the world. Nature has fixed boundaries for summer and winter, day and night, and these limits give the year its meaning. Similarly, economy ensures that fixed limits on expenditures preserve wealth and prevent harm to the people.

However, moderation is necessary even within limitation. If someone imposes overly bitter restrictions on their own nature, they will suffer under them. If one pushes the limitation of others too far, they will rebel. Therefore, boundaries are necessary even within limitation itself.

---

## The Image
> Water is above the lake:
> The image of Limitation.
> Thus the superior person examines number and measure,
> And investigates what virtue and correct conduct are.

A lake is finite; water is inexhaustible. The lake can hold only a specific measure of the infinite water, and this defines its character. In human life, an individual gains significance through differentiation and the establishment of boundaries. The task here is to clearly define these distinctions, which form the backbone of morality. Unlimited possibilities are not suited for human beings; they would cause life to dissolve into the boundless. To grow strong, one requires the voluntary boundaries imposed by duty. Only by surrounding oneself with these limits and freely adopting the mandate of duty does an individual gain significance as a free person.

---

## The Individual Lines

### Nine at the beginning means:
> Not going out of the door and courtyard is no blame.

Often one wishes to undertake a project but faces insurmountable boundaries. In such times, it is essential to understand where to stop. If a person understands this correctly and does not go beyond the limits set for them, they accumulate the energy needed to act decisively when the proper time arrives. Confidentiality is of fundamental importance when preparing significant matters.

Confucius says regarding this: "Where disorder arises, words are the steps that lead to it. If the prince is not confidential, he loses his servant. If the servant is not confidential, he loses his life. If crucial matters are not handled with secrecy in their germinal stage, it harms their fulfillment. Therefore, the superior person is careful to maintain confidentiality and does not go out."

### Nine in the second place means:
> Not going out of the gate and courtyard brings misfortune.

When the time for action comes, one must seize the opportunity immediately. Just as water initially gathers in a lake without flowing out, but surely finds an opening once the lake is full, so it is in human life. It is proper to hesitate as long as the time is not yet ripe, but no longer. Once obstacles are removed and action becomes possible, anxious hesitation is a mistake that inevitably brings misfortune, because the opportunity has been missed.

### Six in the third place means:
> He who knows no limitation will have to lament.
> No blame.

When a person is intent only on pleasure and enjoyment, they easily lose their sense of necessary limits. If they give in to extravagance, they will experience the consequences with regret. One should not try to find fault in others. Only when someone recognizes their own mistake can these unpleasant experiences set them free from faults.

### Six in the fourth place means:
> Contented limitation. Success.

Every limitation has its value. However, if a restriction still requires constant effort, it demands too much energy. When limitation is natural - just as it is in the nature of water to flow downward - it leads inevitably to success because it represents a saving of energy. The force that would otherwise be exhausted in a futile struggle against the object is applied entirely to the task at hand, and success is assured.

### Nine in the fifth place means:
> Sweet limitation brings good fortune.
> Going brings esteem.

Limitation must be carried out in the right way to be effective. If someone only imposes boundaries on others while exempting themselves, those limits will always be felt bitterly and will cause resentment. If, on the other hand, a leader begins the limitation with themselves, expects little from their people, and achieves results with modest means, good fortune follows. Where such an example is set, it finds followers, so that whatever is undertaken must succeed.

### Six at the top means:
> Bitter limitation:
> Perseverance brings misfortune.
> Remorse disappears.

If one is too severe in limitation, people cannot endure it. The more consistent one is in such severity, the worse it becomes, for a reaction cannot be avoided in the long run. Similarly, a tormented body avenges itself when one attempts to proceed with overly strict asceticism. Yet, even if this ruthless severity is not something that can be applied permanently and regularly, there are times when it is the only means to guard against guilt and remorse. These are situations where ruthlessness toward oneself is the sole way to save the soul, which would otherwise perish in halfheartedness and temptation.
