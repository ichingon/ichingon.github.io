### Trigrams
* Above: Zhèn, the Arousing, Thunder
* Below: Xùn, the Gentle, Wind

The strong trigram Zhèn is above, the weak Xùn below. This hexagram is the counterpart to the preceding one: there we find influence, here union as a permanent state. The images are thunder and wind, which are also phenomena naturally paired and permanently connected. The lower trigram indicates gentleness within, while the upper indicates movement without.

Applied to social relations, this represents the institution of marriage as a permanent union of the sexes. While during courtship the young man places himself below the young woman, in marriage - represented by the coming together of the eldest son and the eldest daughter - the husband is the leading and moving force outwardly, while the wife remains gentle and cooperative inwardly.

---

## The Judgment
> Success. No blame.
> Perseverance furthers.
> It furthers one to have somewhere to go.

Duration is a state whose movement is not worn down by obstructions. It is not a state of rest, for mere standstill is regression. Rather, duration is the self-contained, self-renewing movement of an organized, firmly integrated whole that unfolds according to fixed laws, where every end is followed by a new beginning. The end is reached through inward movement - inhalation, systole, concentration. This movement then transitions into a new beginning where the motion is directed outward - exhalation, diastole, expansion.

Thus, the heavenly bodies have their orbits in the sky and can therefore shine permanently. The seasons follow a fixed law of change and transformation, allowing them to exert a lasting influence.

In the same way, the sage finds a lasting meaning in his path, bringing the world to its full realization. By understanding what gives things their duration, one can comprehend the nature of all beings in heaven and on earth.

---

## The Image
> Thunder and wind: the image of Duration.
> Thus the superior man stands firm and does not change his direction.

The thunder rolls and the wind blows. Both are highly volatile phenomena, seemingly the opposite of duration. Yet their appearance and disappearance, their coming and going, follow eternal laws. Similarly, the independence of the superior man does not mean he is rigid and immovable. He always moves with the times and adapts to them. What remains durable is his fixed direction, the inner law of his being that determines all his actions.

## The Lines

### Six at the beginning means:
> Seeking duration too quickly brings misfortune through perseverance.
> Nothing that furthers.

Creating something lasting can only be achieved gradually through long labor and careful reflection. Laozi expresses a similar concept: "If you want to compress something, you must first let it expand properly." Anyone who demands too much all at once acts with headlong haste. Because they attempt too much, they ultimately achieve nothing at all.

### Nine in the second place means:
> Remorse disappears.

The situation is abnormal. The strength of character exceeds the available material power. One might fear being carried away into something beyond one's strength. However, since this is the time of Duration, one succeeds in controlling this inner strength, avoiding any excess and causing all cause for remorse to disappear.

### Nine in the third place means:
> He who does not give duration to his character
> meets with disgrace.
> Persistent humiliation.

When a person is driven by moods aroused by the external world through fear and hope, they lose the inner consistency of their character. Such internal inconsistency leads consistently to painful experiences. These humiliations often come from an unexpected direction. They are not so much the effects of the external world as they are lawful consequences triggered by one's own nature.

### Nine in the fourth place means:
> No game in the field.

To get a shot while hunting, one must go about it the right way. If someone persistently stalks game in a place where none exists, they can wait indefinitely and still find nothing. Duration in seeking is not enough. What is not sought in the right way will not be found.

### Six in the fifth place means:
> Giving duration to one's character through perseverance
> brings good fortune for a woman, misfortune for a man.

A woman should follow one man throughout her entire life, but a man must adhere to his duty at any given time; if he were to constantly adapt himself to the woman, it would be a mistake for him.

Accordingly, a conservative adherence to tradition fits a woman well. A man, by contrast, must remain flexible and adaptable, allowing himself to be guided solely by what his duty demands at the moment.

### Six at the top means:
> Restlessness as a permanent state brings misfortune.

Some people remain in constant, hurried motion without ever attaining inner peace. This restlessness not only prevents any thoroughness but becomes a direct danger when it prevails in a position of authority.
